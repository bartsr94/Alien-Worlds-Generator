World Orogen — Procedural Planet Generator

World Orogen is a free, browser-based procedural planet generator. Build terrestrial and alien planets shaped by tectonic plates, erosion, and volcanism — no download or account required.

How It Works

Start by selecting a world preset (Earth-like, Mars-like, Venus-like, Titan-like, Dead Rock, Ocean World, Ice Ball, Arid Desert, High Gravity, or Random Alien) or use the five Planetary Physics sliders to customize gravity, atmosphere, hydrosphere, base temperature, and axial tilt. Then adjust detail level, plates, and continents, and click "Build New World" to generate a unique planet with realistic continental shapes, mountain ranges, ocean trenches, volcanic island chains, and hotspot volcanism. Every planet is procedurally generated and completely unique.

Key Features

  • Planetary Physics layer — gravity, atmosphere, hydrosphere, base temperature, and axial tilt shape the entire simulation pipeline. Nine world presets produce Mars-like rust deserts, Venus-like hellscapes, Titan-like methane worlds, airless Dead Rock surfaces, and more.
  • Tectonic plate simulation with convergent, divergent, and transform boundaries
  • Multiple erosion types: glacial (fjords, U-shaped valleys), hydraulic (river valleys), and thermal (talus slopes)
  • Climate simulation with seasonal wind patterns, ocean currents, precipitation, and Köppen climate classification
  • Hotspot volcanism with drift-trail island chains (like Hawaii)
  • Interactive plate editing — click any plate to toggle between land and ocean
  • Solar System Mode — explore a 2D Keplerian orrery of our solar system or a procedurally generated alien system; click any rocky or icy world to generate its full globe
  • Multiple visualization modes: terrain, satellite biome, climate, and heightmap
  • 28 detailed inspection layers for geology, atmosphere, ocean, climate, and planetary properties
  • High-resolution equirectangular map export up to 65,536px wide
  • Shareable planet codes — copy a code to share your exact planet with others

Use Cases

World Orogen is used by worldbuilders, game developers, tabletop RPG players, fantasy and sci-fi authors, and anyone who needs realistic terrain. Export heightmaps for use in game engines like Unity or Unreal, or download satellite-style maps for your D&D or sci-fi campaign setting. The planetary physics system lets you create believable alien environments for science fiction settings.

Share Your World

Every planet has a unique planet code. Share the URL (e.g. orogen.studio/#CODE) to let anyone recreate your exact world — including any tectonic plates you've edited by hand.

Welcome to World Orogen

The world's most realistic-ish planet generator — and solar system explorer.

Generate unique procedural planets shaped by tectonic forces — from familiar Earth-like worlds to alien environments like rust deserts, frozen wastelands, methane seas, and crushing hellscapes. Every world is one of a kind.

Click ☉ System in the sidebar to explore an entire solar system — browse our Solar System or generate a random alien one, then click any rocky world to generate its full globe.

Shape Your World

Pick a World Preset (Mars-like, Venus-like, Titan-like, Dead Rock, Ocean World, and more) or use the individual Planetary Physics sliders to set gravity, atmosphere, hydrosphere, temperature, and axial tilt. Use the Shape Your World sliders to control detail, plates, continents, and roughness, then hit Build New World. The Detail slider only changes rendering resolution — continent shapes stay the same, so you can iterate quickly at low detail and crank it up when the planet looks good. Expand Terrain Sculpting to fine-tune erosion and smoothing — these can be reapplied instantly with the ↻ button.

Explore & Edit

Drag to rotate the globe. Scroll to zoom in and out. Ctrl-click any plate to reshape continents — ocean rises into land, land floods into ocean. Hover any region to see elevation, coordinates, temperature, precipitation, and climate classification.

Visualize Your World

Switch between Terrain, Satellite, Climate, and Heightmap views using the tabs under Visual Options. Use the Inspect dropdown for detailed layers like pressure, wind, and precipitation.

At high detail, climate is skipped automatically for faster generation — it computes on demand when you switch to a climate view.

Save & Share

Every planet gets a unique planet code shown below the Build button — including any plates you've edited. Copy it to share with others, or paste a code and click Load to recreate someone else's world. You can also share the URL directly.

Use Export Map to download high-resolution equirectangular images — terrain, satellite, climate, heightmaps, or land masks. Export All downloads satellite, climate, heightmap, and land mask in one click.
